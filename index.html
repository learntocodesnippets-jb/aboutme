Joshna Bhonsle
Joshna Bhonsle
// software engineer · pune, india

I'm a software engineer based in Pune, India, with a background in Java and Python, and a growing interest in AI, machine learning, and cognitive science — particularly how people form and revise beliefs.

Scripture comes first on this site. I read the Bible regularly and write reflections on passages that make me think — about meaning, timing, and how faith and intellectual curiosity go hand in hand. Then comes code: programming learnings, opinions on software practices, and the occasional book note.

I believe good engineering and honest inquiry share the same root: a willingness to be wrong.

CS-6675 · Summer 2025
Advanced Internet Computing
★★★★★

Explores distributed systems, cloud computing, and web-scale architectures. Hands-on projects pushed you to think about fault tolerance and scalability in ways that textbooks don't quite capture.

Distributed SystemsCloudNetworking
CS-6603 · Spring 2025
AI, Ethics & Society
★★★★★

One of the most thought-provoking courses in the program. Covers bias in ML, fairness metrics, accountability, and the societal implications of AI deployment. Essential reading for anyone building AI systems.

AI EthicsPolicyFairness
CS-6400 · Fall 2024
Database Systems Concepts & Design
★★★★★

Solid foundations in relational modeling, SQL, normalization, and query optimization. The semester project — designing a full database system from scratch — was the real learning.

SQLER ModelingOptimization
CS-6035 · Fall 2025
Intro to Information Security
★★★★★

A broad survey of security fundamentals: cryptography, network security, web vulnerabilities, and malware analysis. The CTF-style labs made abstract concepts very concrete.

SecurityCryptographyCTF
CS-6310 · Fall 2025
Software Architecture & Design
★★★★★

UML, design patterns, architectural styles, and trade-off analysis. More conceptual than coding-heavy, but the ability to reason about system structure at scale is genuinely valuable.

ArchitectureDesign PatternsUML
CS-6300 · None
Software Development Process
★★★★★

Agile, Scrum, testing strategies, and software quality. A practical course that maps well to real team workflows. Git-based collaboration assignments were a highlight.

AgileTestingGit
